FOOTSOUL 17’(23rd to 25th March)- GENERAL RULES AND INSTRUCTIONS:

 

1. ALL THE MATCHES SHALL BE PLAYED AND GOVERNED ACCORDING TO THE RULES AND REGULATIONS OF ‘FIFA’ AS ADOPTED FROM TIME TO TIME BY THE ‘ALL INDIA FOOTBALL FEDERATION (AIFF)’ UNLESS OTHERWISE MODIFIED.

 

2. A TEAM’S ROSTER SHOULD NOT HAVE MORE THAN 10 PLAYERS, WITH 7 PLAYERS FORMING THE STARTING LINEUP AND 3 PLAYERS AS SUBSTITUTES.

 

3. THE DURATION OF GAMEPLAY WILL BE 30 MIN. THE 1st AND 2nd HALVES BEING 15 MIN. EACH, WITH A 5 MIN. BREAK IN BETWEEN AS HALF TIME.

 

4. THERE IS NO UPPER LIMIT ON THE NUMBER OF SUBSTITUTIONS IN A SINGLE MATCH. A TEAM CAN MAKE MULTIPLE SUBSTITUTIONS DURING A MATCH WITH CONSENT OF THE REFEREE IN CHARGE (ROLLING SUBSTITUTION).

 

5. DURING A MATCH, IF THE BALL GOES OUT OF PLAY, THE BALL WILL BE KICKED IN, INORDER TO RESUME THE PLAY. THROW IN SHALL NOT BE ALLOWED.

 

6. OFFSIDE WILL NOT BE CONSIDERED DURING THE GAMEPLAY, ALTHOUGH GOAL HANGING WILL BE ACCOUNTED.

 

7. INCASE, IF AT THE END OF 2nd HALF BOTH TEAMS HAVE THE SAME SCORE, THEN PENALTY SHOOT OUT (ONE –STEP) WILL BE CONDUCTED.

 

8. FOOTSOUL IS A KNOCKOUT TOURNAMENT.

 

9. MATCHES WILL BE CONDUCTED AT DTU SPORTS COMPLEX (FOOTBALL FIELD).

 

10. REPORTING TIME IS 30 MIN PRIOR TO THE START OF THE MATCH. IF THE ATTENDANCE IS NOT REGISTERED 15 MINUTES BEFORE THE MATCH, OPPONENT TEAM WILL BE AWARDED A BY.

 

11. BIBS WILL BE PROVIDED BY THE ORGANISERS.

 

12. ANY KIND OF INDISCIPLINARY ACTIVITIES IN THE CAMPUS WILL RESULT IN DISQUALIFICATION.

 

13. THE DTU CAMPUS IS A STRICTLY NO-SMOKING ZONE.

 

14. ALL THE PLAYERS MUST BRING THEIR ID CARD, WITHOUT WHICH ENTRY INTO THE CAMPUS WILL NOT BE PERMITTED.

 

ENTRY FEES FOR EACH TEAM IS 1500 INR.

TOURNAMENT WINNER WILL BE AWARDED 25000 INR.

 

Note: The organizing committee has the right to make changes in the rules and notifying the affected teams if required, the final prize money awarded is subjected to the number of teams participating.

Rules:

  • All FIDE Rules apply as on 1st Jan 2017 unless otherwise mentioned.
  • The basic pairing system shall be Controlled Swiss System Pairings using Match Points for both Pairing and Scoring.
  • Time control: 25 minutes plus an additional 10 second increment per move.
  • Number of rounds will be decided depending on the number of participating teams.
  • Chess clocks shall be provided to top boards in cases on shortage of clocks. Participants can use their own chess clocks for the matches.
  • A team must consist of a maximum of 5 players, with 4 players playing in the tournament and one player playing as a substitute.
  • There shall be no restriction to including women players in the Chess Team.
  • Each team must clearly state the playing order of its team while sending the entry. If the team wishes to play their substitute in a particular round, the Team Captain should intimate this to the Chief Arbiter before the round begins. Rules for substitution have been explained below in detail.
  • Each team is required to play through the entire tournament. Teams which do not play two rounds in succession shall be automatically disqualified and their name shall be struck off from the pairing list.
  • Each team shall be awarded 1 point for a win, 0.5 points for a 2-2 draw and 0 points in case of a loss. In case of the same number of points of two winning teams, the winner shall be adjudged according to FIDE guidelines.
  • The final positions of the teams shall be established by the number of game points scored by each team.
  • Only one participating team is permitted from a single college. The host college shall have 2 participating teams in the tournament.
  • In case of any dispute, the decision of the Chief Arbiter shall be considered final and binding.

    • Rules for substitution:

  • "Roll over substitution"
  • The boards lower to the board on which substitution is made will slide up one board till the board of substitution. Example 1: Assume the following board order- A-1, B-2, C-3, D-4. Substitute player E. In case, the player A (on board 1) is to be substituted, the new board order should be B-1, C-2, D-3, E-4 (Non- playing A). Example 2: Assume following board order- A-1, B-2, C-3, D-4. Substitute player E. In case the player C (on board 3) is to be substituted, the new board order should be A-1 B-2, D- 3, E-4 (Non playing-C). No player shall be replaced after the team has been registered.
